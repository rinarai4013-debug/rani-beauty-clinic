# Washington State Med Spa Regulations
## Source: WA DOH - Information and Guidance for Operating a Med Spa
## URL: https://doh.wa.gov/licenses-permits-and-certificates/professions-new-renew-or-update/medical-spa-and-esthetic-services-work-group/

## Key Regulatory Points

### Business Structure
- Corporate practice of medicine doctrine applies - restrictions on who can OWN a medspa
- Prohibitions against fee-splitting, rebating, corporate practice of medicine, aiding/abetting unlicensed practice
- Consult an attorney for ownership structure (corporation, LLC, partnership, sole proprietorship)

### Medical Director Requirements
- Must have a designated medical director who is a licensed physician (MD, DO, or PA)
- WMC (Washington Medical Commission) regulates MDs and PAs
- BOMS (Board of Osteopathic Medicine and Surgery) regulates DOs

### Laser/Device Rules
- WAC 246-919-605: Laser rule for MDs
- WAC 246-853-630: Laser rule for DOs
- WAC 246-918-125: Laser rule for PAs
- Rules describe when MD/DO can delegate procedures to other licensed professionals and required supervision level

### Nonsurgical Medical Cosmetic Procedures
- WAC 246-919-606: Nonsurgical medical cosmetic procedures rule for MDs
- Covers injection of substances for cosmetic purposes and prescription devices (non-laser)
- Describes delegation and supervision requirements

### Medication Administration
- Anyone administering medication must have legal authority to do so
- IV hydration bag = medication → requires medication order before initiating
- Pharmacy Commission regulates pharmaceutical distribution

### Scope of Practice
- Critical to understand varying legal scopes of practice for each licensed person
- Allowing a person to exceed their scope = disciplinary action against medical director, supervisor, AND individual
