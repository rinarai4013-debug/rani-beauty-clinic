UnitedHealthcare Policy Overview

UnitedHealthcare covers Ozempic and Mounjaro as Tier 2-3 specialty medications for type 2 diabetes management across commercial, Medicare Advantage, and employer plans in Washington. Both medications require prior authorization through OptumRx, UnitedHealthcare's pharmacy benefit manager.

Plan Types and Coverage
Commercial/Employer Plans: Standard PA requirements apply
Medicare Advantage: Subject to Medicare Part D regulations with UHC-specific criteria
Medicaid (Apple Health): Coverage varies; check with Washington Apple Health directly

The medications are not typically covered for weight loss alone under diabetes NDC codes, though this may change as FDA expands indications.

Medical Necessity Requirements
Primary Indication: Type 2 Diabetes

UnitedHealthcare approves these medications when prescribed for type 2 diabetes with evidence of:

A1C ≥6.5% (within 90 days), OR
Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL, OR
2-hour oral glucose tolerance test ≥200 mg/dL, OR
Random plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL with hyperglycemia symptoms

For patients diagnosed more than 2 years ago, medical records confirming ongoing type 2 diabetes treatment are sufficient.

Note: BMI and cardiovascular risk factors are not explicitly required in UnitedHealthcare's current policy for diabetes coverage.
Step Therapy and Exceptions
Standard Step Therapy Requirements

UnitedHealthcare typically requires trial of first-line diabetes medications before approving GLP-1 receptor agonists:

Metformin (unless contraindicated)
Additional oral antidiabetics (sulfonylureas, SGLT2 inhibitors)
Basal insulin (in some cases)
Medical Exception Pathways

You can bypass step therapy if:

Previous medications failed to achieve glycemic control
Contraindications exist (e.g., kidney disease, allergies)
Drug interactions prevent use of alternatives
Patient experienced intolerable side effects

Documentation needed: Pharmacy records, provider notes detailing reasons for failure/intolerance, and clinical rationale for bypassing standard therapy.

Appeals Process in Washington

Washington state provides robust consumer protections for insurance denials through a two-tiered system: internal appeals followed by external review.

Internal Appeals with UnitedHealthcare

First Level - Reconsideration

Timeline: Submit within 12 months of denial
Method: UHC Provider Portal, phone (800-711-4555), or mail
Decision: 10 business days
Required: All clinical documentation, provider support

Second Level - Formal Appeal

Timeline: After first-level denial
Process: Includes peer-to-peer physician review option
Decision: 15-30 days
Expedited: 72 hours for urgent medical needs
External Review via Washington IRO Process

After exhausting internal appeals, Washington residents can request Independent Review Organization (IRO) review under RCW 48.43.535.

Key Details:

Eligibility: Final internal adverse determination for non-grandfathered plans
Timeline: File within 4 months of internal denial (72 hours for expedited)
Process: Office of the Insurance Commissioner assigns independent IRO
Decision: 45 days standard, 18 days expedited
Outcome: Binding on UnitedHealthcare if overturned

How to File:

Submit Form H-46A to: Office of the Insurance Commissioner, External Review Unit, PO Box 40255, Olympia, WA 98504-0255
Fax: (360) 586-3535
Online portal: insurance.wa.gov
Help line: (800) 562-6900
Tip: Washington's external review process has approximately 70% overturn rate for medical necessity denials. The independent medical experts often side with patients when proper clinical evidence is provided.

FAQ

How long does UnitedHealthcare prior authorization take in Washington? Standard review takes 15 business days. Expedited review (for urgent medical needs) takes 72 hours. Automated approvals through OptumRx PreCheck can process in under 30 seconds for eligible cases.

What if Ozempic or Mounjaro is non-formulary on my plan? You can request a formulary exception with clinical justification. If denied, appeal through internal and external review processes. The IRO can override plan restrictions if medically necessary.

Can I request expedited appeals in Washington? Yes, for urgent medical situations where standard timelines could jeopardize your health. Submit expedited requests within 72 hours with documentation of medical urgency.

Does step therapy apply if I tried medications outside Washington? Yes, prior medication history from other states counts toward step therapy requirements. Provide pharmacy records and provider documentation of previous trials and outcomes.

What happens if my employer plan is self-funded? Self-funded ERISA plans may not be subject to Washington state external review laws. Contact the U.S. Department of Labor for ERISA plan appeals guidance, though many employers voluntarily use IRO processes.

How much does external review cost in Washington? External review through the Office of the Insurance Commissioner is free for consumers. The IRO process is funded through insurer fees, not patient costs.

Can I continue current medication during appeals? Washington law allows continued coverage during appeals in some circumstances. Contact the Office of the Insurance Commissioner at (800) 562-6900 for guidance on your specific situation.
