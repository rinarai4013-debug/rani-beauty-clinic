# WAC 246-919-605 — Laser/LLRP Devices (MDs)
- LLRP = laser, light, radiofrequency, plasma devices (FDA prescription devices)
- Use of LLRP device = practice of medicine (RCW 18.71.011)
- Physician must: take history, physical exam, diagnosis, treatment plan, informed consent (including disclosure that nonphysician may operate device), emergency/follow-up instructions, prepare medical record
- Physician must have written office protocol for each LLRP device
- Quality assurance program required
- **Delegation rules:**
  - Physician must be on-site for INITIAL treatment
  - For subsequent treatments: physician must be reachable by phone and able to see patient within 60 minutes; local back-up physician must agree in writing
  - Back-up physician absence must be "brief and intermittent" — NOT an ongoing arrangement
  - Supervised professional must follow written protocol, no independent medical judgment

# WAC 246-919-606 — Nonsurgical Medical Cosmetic Procedures (Botox, Fillers, etc.)
- Covers: injection of medications/substances for cosmetic purposes, use of prescription devices (non-laser)
- Does NOT cover: surgery, lasers (covered by 246-919-605), nonprescription devices, IV therapy
- Physician must: history, physical exam, diagnosis, treatment plan, informed consent, emergency/follow-up instructions, medical record
- **Delegation allowed to: PA, RN, LPN only** (NOT estheticians for injectables)
- Delegate must have training in: techniques, cutaneous medicine, indications/contraindications, pre/post care, complications, infection control
- Written office protocol required
- Patient must sign consent listing foreseeable side effects AND identity/license of delegate
- Delegate must wear name tag identifying their license
- **On-site requirement:**
  - FDA-APPROVED substances (e.g., Botox, Juvederm): physician need NOT be on-site; must be reachable by phone and able to respond within 30 minutes
  - NON-FDA-APPROVED or off-label substances: physician must be ON-SITE for entire duration
