## Executive Summary

This playbook provides Rani Medspa with a complete, actionable Meta advertising strategy for 2025–2026. It is built around a $3,000/month budget and covers every dimension of a high-performing paid social program: budget allocation by campaign objective, treatment-specific audience definitions, lookalike and retargeting architecture, creative briefs for each ad format, landing page conversion requirements, Seattle market benchmarks, a competitor intelligence report from the Meta Ad Library, a month-by-month seasonal campaign calendar, and a video content strategy for Reels.

The core strategic principle is **treatment-specificity over brand genericism**. Generic "Visit Our MedSpa" campaigns consistently underperform compared to campaigns built around individual treatments with tailored audiences, creative, and offers. Splitting a single medspa campaign into treatment-specific campaigns typically drops the average cost per lead (CPL) by 40–60% [^1].

## 3. Lookalike Audience Strategy

Lookalike audiences (LLAs) allow Meta's algorithm to find new users who share the behavioral and demographic characteristics of your best existing patients. The source audience quality is the single most important factor in LLA performance.

### Recommended Source Audiences (Ranked by Priority)

**Tier 1 — Highest Value Sources**

1. **High-LTV Patient List (1% LLA):** Upload a CSV of patients who have spent $2,000+ or visited 3+ times in the past 12 months. This is the most powerful source audience because it reflects your ideal, loyal patient. Build a 1% LLA for prospecting and a 2–3% LLA for broader reach.

2. **Membership Holders (1% LLA):** Patients enrolled in a recurring membership program represent the highest lifetime value and strongest brand affinity. A 1% LLA from this list will find users most likely to commit to ongoing treatments.

3. **Recent Purchasers — Last 90 Days (1–2% LLA):** Upload patients who have booked and completed a treatment in the last 90 days. This recency ensures the algorithm uses fresh behavioral signals.

**Tier 2 — Supplementary Sources**

4. **Website Visitors — Purchase/Booking Page (1% LLA):** Create a custom audience of users who visited the booking confirmation page (indicating a completed booking), then build a 1% LLA from this group.

5. **Video Viewers — 75%+ Completion (1–2% LLA):** Users who watched most of a treatment video have demonstrated high intent. This is an excellent source for finding similar high-intent cold audiences.

6. **Instagram Engagers — Last 90 Days (1–3% LLA):** Useful for building brand awareness campaigns when the above lists are too small (minimum 100 users required for LLA creation; 1,000+ recommended).

### Treatment-Specific LLA Architecture

Rather than building one generic "medspa patient" LLA, create separate lookalike audiences for each major treatment category. A Botox patient and a body contouring patient have different demographics, income levels, and motivations. Separate LLAs allow the algorithm to find the right person for each specific campaign [^1].

## 4. Retargeting Strategy

Retargeting is the highest-efficiency spend in the Meta ads ecosystem. By targeting users who have already interacted with Rani Medspa, the cost per acquisition drops significantly compared to cold prospecting — in some cases by up to 60% [^2].

### Retargeting Audience Layers (Prioritized)

**Layer 1 — Hottest Intent (Highest Priority)**
- Website visitors who viewed a specific treatment page but did not book (last 30 days)
- Users who clicked a "Book Now" or "Learn More" CTA but did not complete the booking form (last 14 days)
- *Ad Strategy:* Show a direct offer ad with a clear price point and a single CTA. Address the most common objection for that specific treatment (e.g., "Wondering if Botox will look natural? Here's what 20 units actually looks like.").

**Layer 2 — Warm Engagement**
- Website visitors (all pages, last 60 days)
- Instagram profile visitors (last 30 days)
- Users who engaged with a Facebook or Instagram post (liked, commented, saved, shared — last 90 days)
- *Ad Strategy:* Show testimonial videos and social proof. Reinforce trust and introduce a limited-time offer to create urgency.

**Layer 3 — Video Viewers**
- Users who watched 50%+ of any video ad (last 60 days)
- *Ad Strategy:* Show a consultation offer or a "next step" ad. These users are educated about the treatment; the goal is to move them to book.

**Layer 4 — Email List Segments**
- Upload email list of leads who have not yet booked (non-purchaser suppression list)
- Upload email list of lapsed patients (no visit in 6+ months)
- *Ad Strategy:* For non-bookers, offer an exclusive first-visit incentive. For lapsed patients, use a "We miss you" re-engagement offer or promote a new treatment they haven't tried.

### Retargeting Creative Principles
- Never show the same ad to a retargeting audience that you showed them in prospecting. The user has already seen it; show them something new that moves them further down the funnel.
- Use frequency caps (2–3 impressions per week per user) to avoid ad fatigue.
- Exclude users who have booked in the last 30 days from all retargeting campaigns.

## 5. Ad Formats & Creative Strategy (2025–2026)

The most effective medspa ad creative in 2025–2026 is characterized by authenticity, education, and clear outcome-driven messaging. Generic stock imagery of women in robes consistently underperforms compared to real patient content and provider-led educational videos [^3].

### Top-Performing Ad Formats (Ranked by CPL Efficiency)

| Format | CPL Impact | Best Use Case | Platform Placement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Short-Form Video (15–30s Reel) | Lowest CPL | Treatment education, provider intros, UGC | Reels, Stories, Feed |
| UGC Video (Patient-Generated) | Very Low CPL | Trust-building, social proof, journey content | Reels, Stories, Feed |
| Carousel (3–5 images/videos) | Low–Medium CPL | Multiple treatment areas, step-by-step process, package options | Feed |
| Single Image with Offer Overlay | Medium CPL | Time-sensitive promotions, seasonal specials | Feed, Stories |
| Lead Form Ad (Meta Instant Form) | Lowest CPL (but lower quality) | High-volume lead gen for Botox, facials, consultations | Feed |

Short-form vertical video (9:16 ratio) performs 20–30% better on CPL in Stories and Reels placements compared to square or horizontal feed formats [^1]. Always create vertical versions of all video content.

### Video Ad Strategy for Reels

Instagram Reels is the highest-priority placement for medspa video ads in 2025–2026. Short-form videos generate 2–3x higher engagement than static images in the beauty and wellness category [^3].

**Content Types That Perform Best for Medspa Reels:**
1. **"Day in the Life" Treatment Clips (10–20 seconds):** Quick, satisfying clips of a treatment being performed (e.g., a HydraFacial extraction, a lip filler injection, a laser pass). These are highly shareable and drive organic reach.
2. **Patient Reaction Videos:** Capturing a patient's genuine reaction when they see their results for the first time. Emotional authenticity drives engagement and shares.
3. **Educational "Myth Busting" Videos:** "Does Botox make you look frozen? Let me show you what natural Botox actually looks like." These build trust and address objections simultaneously.
4. **"Get Ready With Me" Treatment Prep:** A patient showing up to their appointment, going through the process, and leaving glowing. Normalizes the experience for first-timers.
5. **Provider Personality Content:** The injector or esthetician sharing their philosophy, answering common questions, or showing a day in the clinic. Builds the personal brand that drives patient loyalty.

**Reels Production Guidelines:**
- Film in vertical (9:16) format
- Always include captions (85% of Reels are watched without sound)
- Hook must land in the first 1–2 seconds
- Keep under 30 seconds for best completion rates
- Use trending audio when appropriate (increases organic distribution)
- Branding should be subtle — feel native, not like an ad

## 6. Landing Page Best Practices for Medspa Ads

A high-converting landing page is the difference between a $15 lead and a $150 lead. The most common failure mode is driving paid traffic to a generic homepage or a treatment page that was designed for SEO, not conversion [^4].

### The 9 Elements of a High-Converting Medspa Landing Page [^4]

1. **A headline that mirrors the patient's desire, not the treatment name.** Example: "Smooth Away Fine Lines and Look Refreshed — Without Anyone Knowing" rather than "Botox Treatments in Seattle."
2. **A subheadline that confirms they're in the right place.** Example: "Rani Medspa — Seattle's trusted injectors for natural, beautiful results."
3. **A short value proposition explaining what results to expect.** Two to three sentences maximum. Focus on outcomes, not features.
4. **Real social proof that feels genuine.** Video testimonials from real patients are the gold standard. Google review widgets and star ratings also convert well.
5. **Before-and-after photos with honest context.** Include the number of units, sessions, or syringes used. Patients trust specificity.
6. **A "What to Expect" section.** Walk through the appointment step by step. This reduces anxiety and pre-qualifies patients, leading to higher show rates.
7. **Transparent pricing or a price range.** Hidden pricing is the single biggest cause of bounce on medspa landing pages. Display starting prices or promotional offers clearly.
8. **A soft, personal call-to-action.** "Book a Free 15-Minute Consultation" outperforms "Book Now" for high-consideration treatments. Multiple CTA buttons throughout the page (not just at the bottom) are essential.
9. **A friendly FAQ section.** Address the final objections: "Is it painful?", "How long does it last?", "What is the downtime?" Answering these on the page reduces phone call volume and increases online bookings.

### Elements to Avoid
- Walls of medical jargon or ingredient lists
- Stock photos of models who don't represent real patients
- Navigation menus that lead visitors away from the page
- A single CTA button buried at the bottom

### Lead Form vs. Landing Page Decision Framework

| Use Meta Lead Forms When... | Use Landing Pages When... |
| :--- | :--- |
| Promoting Botox, facials, or simple consultations | Promoting body contouring, PRP, or high-ticket packages |
| Your team can call/text leads within 5 minutes | You want to pre-qualify leads before they call |
| Maximizing lead volume is the primary goal | Lead quality matters more than lead volume |
| CPL is the primary optimization metric | You have a strong nurture sequence (email/SMS) |

Lead form CPL is typically 30–50% lower than landing page CPL, but the leads are less qualified. A split approach — lead forms for Botox and facials, landing pages for body contouring and premium packages — is recommended [^1].

## 8. Competitor Intelligence Report (Meta Ad Library Analysis)

The following analysis is based on a direct review of the Meta Ad Library for Seattle-area medspa competitors conducted in March 2026.

### Competitor 1: Element MedSpa (Seattle, WA)
**Active Ads:** 4 active campaigns (as of March 2026)  
**Strategy:** Element MedSpa is the most active Meta advertiser among Seattle competitors reviewed. Their strategy is heavily centered on short-form educational video (Reels), running exclusively on Instagram. They explain the science behind treatments in accessible language (e.g., "Salmon DNA injections...deliver DNA-repairing polynucleotides directly into the dermis") and consistently state clear pricing in the ad copy itself (e.g., "$450/session" for microneedling, "$650 for Half Syringe Lips + 20 Units Tox"). They use a "Send Message" CTA on Instagram, routing leads directly to DMs.  
**Creative Style:** Conversational, science-forward, transparent on pricing, before-and-after video for lip fillers.  
**Opportunity for Rani:** Element focuses heavily on Instagram only. Rani can gain an advantage by running on both Facebook and Instagram, targeting an older demographic (35–55) on Facebook that Element may be missing.

### Competitor 2: Sheer Complexions, LLC
**Active Ads:** 1 active campaign (as of March 2026)  
**Strategy:** Running a monthly specials promotion on Facebook. The ad is a static graphic listing multiple offers: "Tox Lock-In for $179, Glow-Up Package for $299, Buy More Get More Gift Card Event." The approach is discount-heavy and promotion-focused.  
**Creative Style:** Graphic-heavy, text-forward, discount-oriented.  
**Opportunity for Rani:** Sheer Complexions is competing on price. Rani can differentiate by competing on experience, trust, and results rather than discounts, which attracts higher-LTV patients.

### Competitor 3: Complexions Rx
**Active Ads:** 1 active campaign (low impressions, as of March 2026)  
**Strategy:** Running a high-value lead generation offer for a specific concern (Hair Restoration): "Free Hair Loss Assessment + $600 Off Treatment." Uses a clear before-and-after image of hair density improvement. The offer is structured as a voucher with artificial scarcity ("50 vouchers available").  
**Creative Style:** Problem-solution framing, strong offer with clear value, urgency mechanics.  
**Opportunity for Rani:** This voucher/lead magnet approach is effective for high-ticket treatments. Rani could adapt this strategy for body contouring (e.g., "Free Body Mapping Consultation + $200 Off Your First Session").

### Competitor 4: SkinSpirit (National Chain with Seattle Locations)
**Strategy (Based on General Research):** SkinSpirit runs polished, brand-forward campaigns emphasizing their clinical expertise and national reputation. They tend to use high-production-value imagery and focus on brand trust rather than direct-response offers.  
**Opportunity for Rani:** As a local, independent medspa, Rani can out-compete SkinSpirit on the "personal relationship" and "community" dimensions. UGC content and provider personality videos will feel more authentic than a national chain's polished brand ads.

### Competitor 5: General Seattle Medspa Market
Most smaller Seattle medspas are not running active Meta ads, or are running very low-impression campaigns. This represents a significant opportunity. The Seattle medspa market is relatively under-advertised on Meta compared to markets like Los Angeles, Miami, or New York, meaning CPMs and CPLs should be at or below national averages.

### Competitive Gaps & Rani's Differentiation Opportunities

| Competitor Weakness | Rani's Opportunity |

| Sheer Complexions: Discount-heavy, low brand equity | Compete on results, experience, and trust — not price |

## 10. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) & Measurement Framework

To manage the $3,000/month budget effectively, track the following KPIs weekly and optimize accordingly.

| KPI | Target | Action if Below Target |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cost Per Lead (CPL) | < $35 average | Test new creative hooks; review audience targeting |
| Lead-to-Consultation Rate | > 30% | Improve follow-up speed; review lead form questions |
| Consultation-to-Booking Rate | > 50% | Review consultation process; improve offer clarity |
| Cost Per Booked Patient | < $100 | Optimize full funnel, not just ad creative |
| ROAS | > 3:1 | Shift budget to highest-performing treatment campaigns |
| CTR | > 2% | Test new creative; refresh ad copy |
| Ad Frequency | < 3.5 per week | Rotate creative; expand audience size |
