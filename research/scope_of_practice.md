# Scope of Practice — Washington State Medspa Staff

## MD (Dr. Landfield)
- Full scope: diagnosis, treatment, prescribing, performing all procedures
- Must perform initial patient evaluation before delegating any cosmetic procedure
- Ultimately responsible for all patient safety regardless of who performs procedure
- Must maintain written office protocols for all delegated procedures
- Must maintain quality assurance program
- Must document training records for all LLRP devices

## RN (Registered Nurse)
Source: WABON Advisory Opinion NCAO 27.00; RCW 18.79.040
- CAN: Administer Botox, Dysport, Xeomin, dermal fillers under physician delegation
- CAN: Perform laser/LLRP procedures under physician delegation (WAC 246-919-605)
- CAN: Administer IM injections (B12, testosterone, GLP-1) under physician order
- CAN: Draw blood (venipuncture)
- Does NOT need physician physically present for FDA-approved injectables (physician must be reachable within 30 min)
- Must follow written office protocols
- Must document care provided
- Must demonstrate competency (training records required)
- Must wear name tag identifying license

## LPN (Licensed Practical Nurse)
Source: WABON Advisory Opinion NCAO 27.00; RCW 18.79.060, 18.79.270
- CAN: Administer cosmetic injectables under physician delegation
- CAN: Administer IM injections under physician order
- More limited scope than RN; must work under direction of authorized practitioner or RN
- Does NOT need physician physically present for FDA-approved substances

## ARNP/NP (Advanced Registered Nurse Practitioner)
Source: RCW 18.79.250; WAC 246-840-300
- Full prescriptive authority in Washington (full practice authority state)
- CAN: Diagnose, treat, prescribe independently
- CAN: Perform and delegate cosmetic procedures
- CAN: Serve as medical director (if clinic owner or authorized)
- CAN: Own a medspa independently

## Esthetician (Basic)
Source: RCW 18.16.020(27); DOL Laser Rules
- CAN: Skin care, facials, waxing, chemical peels (superficial/light only), microdermabrasion
- CAN: Use FDA "over the counter" devices only
- CANNOT: Administer any injections (Botox, fillers, B12, GLP-1) — explicitly prohibited
- CANNOT: Use FDA prescription devices (lasers, IPL, RF) independently
- CANNOT: Perform medium or deep chemical peels
- Under physician supervision: CAN use prescription LLRP devices IF properly trained

## Master Esthetician
Source: RCW 18.16.020(29); DOL Laser Rules
- All basic esthetician scope PLUS:
- CAN: Perform medium-depth chemical peels
- CAN: Use FDA prescription medical devices (lasers, IPL, RF, plasma, ultrasound) under physician delegation
- Must comply with WAC 246-919-605 delegation requirements
- CANNOT: Administer any injections — explicitly prohibited by RCW 18.16.020(27)
- Must be under physician supervision for prescription devices

## KEY RULE: Injections
Under NO circumstances can an esthetician (basic or master) administer injections.
Only RNs, LPNs, PAs, ARNPs, and MDs can administer injections in a medspa setting.
