## Executive Summary

This creative brief is the strategic foundation for all visual content production at Rani Beauty Clinic. It establishes the brand's visual identity, outlines the creative direction for photography and video, provides a comprehensive Reels and TikTok video concept library, defines the User-Generated Content (UGC) strategy, guides the use of AI image generation as a stock photo alternative, and recommends the equipment required for consistent, high-quality self-produced content.

Every image and video produced under this brief should reinforce a single, unified brand promise: **Rani Beauty Clinic is where clinical expertise meets elevated, personalised care.**

### 1.3 Lighting Philosophy

Lighting is the single most important technical element in aesthetic clinic photography. The following principles must be applied consistently across all content:

**Natural Light First.** Whenever possible, position subjects near large windows and use natural, diffused daylight as the primary light source. Overcast days produce the most flattering, even light for skin tones. Avoid direct, harsh sunlight that creates unflattering shadows.

**Soft, Directional Artificial Light.** When natural light is insufficient, use large softboxes positioned at a 45-degree angle to the subject. This creates a gentle, dimensional light that flatters skin and avoids the flat, clinical look of direct flash.

**Golden Hour for Exterior and Lifestyle Shots.** Schedule exterior clinic shots and lifestyle images during the 30-minute window before sunset. The warm, amber light during this period aligns perfectly with the brand's warm, elevated aesthetic.

**Consistent Lighting for Before/After Photography.** This is non-negotiable. Before and after images must be captured under identical lighting conditions, at the same distance, and with the same camera settings. Use a ring light or beauty dish positioned at the same height and angle for every standardised shot. Mark the floor with tape to ensure exact positioning is replicated.

## Part 2: Photography Direction

### 2.1 Shot Composition Principles

**The Rule of Thirds.** Position key subjects — a provider's face, a product, a client's glowing skin — at the intersection points of a 3×3 grid rather than dead-centre. This creates more dynamic, visually engaging images.

**Depth of Field.** Use a wide aperture (f/1.8–f/2.8) for portrait and detail shots to create a soft, blurred background that isolates the subject and communicates luxury. Reserve deeper depth of field (f/5.6–f/8) for flat-lays and room establishing shots where all elements should be in focus.

**Leading Lines.** Use the natural lines of the clinic — corridors, treatment bed edges, product arrangements — to draw the viewer's eye toward the primary subject.

**Negative Space.** Do not overcrowd the frame. Ample negative space (empty, clean background) communicates luxury and allows the subject to breathe. This is particularly important for product and detail shots.

### 2.2 Wardrobe Direction

Three distinct wardrobe "looks" should be prepared for the shoot day to maximise the variety of usable images while minimising time spent changing:

**Look 1 — The Lifestyle Look:** Neutral, fitted clothing in cream, blush, or sage tones. Used for headshots, candid lifestyle shots, and consultation scenes. Accessories such as simple jewellery and a structured bag are encouraged.

**Look 2 — The Professional Look:** The same base outfit with the addition of a structured blazer in a neutral or brand-aligned colour. This elevates the look for website hero images, LinkedIn, and press kit usage.

**Look 3 — The Clinical Look:** A clean white lab coat layered over the base outfit, paired with branded gloves for treatment shots. This is the most-used look for social media content as it directly communicates clinical expertise.

### 2.3 Model & Consent Protocol

All photography and video featuring identifiable individuals — whether staff, models, or real clients — requires documented consent prior to the shoot. The following consent documents must be obtained and stored:

- **Photography & Video Release Form:** Grants the clinic the right to use images and video for marketing purposes.
- **Social Media Use Consent:** Specifically grants permission to post content on Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms.
- **Before/After Consent Form:** A separate, specific consent form for any before/after imagery, which must clearly state the platforms and contexts in which the images will be used.

The **Consent & Model Tracker** sheet in the accompanying Shot List Spreadsheet should be completed for every individual who appears in any content produced.

## Part 3: Video Content Plan — 10 Reels & TikTok Concepts Per Service

The following 10 video concepts form the complete content framework for Rani Beauty Clinic's short-form video strategy. Each concept is designed to be produced for every major service offered at the clinic, creating a deep, evergreen library of content. The **Video Content Calendar Spreadsheet** maps these concepts to a specific 12-week posting schedule.

### Concept 1: The Walk-Through (POV / Experiential)
**Purpose:** To reduce anxiety and build excitement for prospective clients by showing them exactly what to expect before they ever book an appointment.
**Format:** A first-person, point-of-view video that follows the client journey from the clinic's front door through to settling into the treatment chair. The camera should move at a calm, unhurried pace to convey the relaxed, welcoming atmosphere.
**Duration:** 30–45 seconds. **Audio:** Calming, luxury lo-fi instrumental.

### Concept 2: Behind-the-Scenes Prep (ASMR / Detail)
**Purpose:** To satisfy curiosity about the treatment process and reinforce the clinic's commitment to sterile, meticulous preparation.
**Format:** A close-up, ASMR-style video featuring the satisfying sounds and visuals of the provider preparing the treatment tray — gloves snapping, sealed products being opened, instruments being arranged. No music; natural sounds only.
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds. **Audio:** Natural ASMR sounds.

### Concept 3: The "Why" (Provider Education — Talking Head)
**Purpose:** To establish the provider as a trusted authority and educate the audience on the specific benefits, ideal candidates, and realistic expectations for each treatment.
**Format:** The lead provider speaks directly to camera in a confident, conversational tone. B-roll of the treatment is intercut throughout. A lavalier microphone is essential for crisp, professional audio.
**Duration:** 30–60 seconds. **Audio:** Direct audio (lavalier mic) with a subtle background track.

### Concept 4: Treatment in Action (Cinematic / Slow-Motion)
**Purpose:** To showcase the artistry, precision, and technology behind each treatment in a visually compelling way that stops the scroll.
**Format:** High-quality, slow-motion footage (120fps) of the treatment being performed. Focus on the provider's hands, the device in action, and the client's comfortable expression. This is the most technically demanding concept and requires the highest production value.
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds. **Audio:** Trending cinematic or instrumental audio.

### Concept 5: What to Expect (Step-by-Step with Text Overlays)
**Purpose:** To demystify the treatment process and address common first-timer anxieties by walking the viewer through each step.
**Format:** B-roll footage of each stage of the appointment, with clear, bold text overlays labelling each step (e.g., "Step 1: Consultation," "Step 2: Skin Prep," "Step 3: Treatment," "Step 4: Aftercare"). Pre-plan the text overlays before filming to ensure the B-roll matches the script.
**Duration:** 30–45 seconds. **Audio:** Upbeat, engaging trending sound.

### Concept 6: Before & After Transformation (Transition / Reveal)
**Purpose:** To provide compelling visual proof of results and drive booking intent.
**Format:** A smooth transition from the before image to the after image. This can be achieved through a split-screen, a wipe transition, or a trending "reveal" audio cue. Standardised photography (identical lighting, angle, and distance) is critical for the impact of this concept. Full written consent is mandatory.
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds. **Audio:** Trending transformation or reveal audio.

### Concept 7: The Post-Treatment Glow (Lifestyle / Reaction)
**Purpose:** To capture the authentic emotional payoff of the treatment — the moment the client sees their results — which is the most powerful social proof available.
**Format:** Film the client's genuine, unscripted reaction as they look in the mirror immediately after treatment. Follow this with a close-up of their glowing skin. Do not over-direct; the authenticity of the moment is the asset.
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds. **Audio:** Upbeat, feel-good trending audio.

### Concept 8: Aftercare Essentials (Flat-lay / Stop Motion)
**Purpose:** To demonstrate the clinic's commitment to post-treatment care and to showcase the premium recovery products available for purchase.
**Format:** A visually appealing stop-motion or time-lapse of the aftercare products being placed, one by one, into the branded recovery bag. Each product should be clearly visible and identifiable. Use the brand's marble flat-lay surface and botanical props.
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds. **Audio:** Crisp, rhythmic beat.

### Concept 9: Myth vs. Fact (Educational / Engaging)
**Purpose:** To proactively address and dismantle the most common misconceptions and fears that prevent prospective clients from booking, thereby removing barriers to conversion.
**Format:** The provider delivers three myth-and-fact pairs directly to camera, with bold text pop-ups reinforcing each point. Research the top three misconceptions for each specific service before filming.
**Duration:** 30–45 seconds. **Audio:** Trending audio with text pop-up sound effects.

### Concept 10: Day in the Life (Vlog Style)
**Purpose:** To humanise the brand, showcase the team's expertise and passion, and demonstrate the high demand for the clinic's services.
**Format:** A fast-paced, energetic montage of a full day (or multiple days) performing a specific treatment on multiple clients. Intercut provider close-ups, client reactions, and clinic ambiance shots. This concept requires filming across multiple appointments to achieve sufficient variety.
**Duration:** 30–60 seconds. **Audio:** Energetic, fast-paced trending audio.

## Part 4: User-Generated Content (UGC) Strategy

User-generated content is the most credible form of social proof available to an aesthetic clinic. Prospective clients trust the unfiltered experiences of real clients far more than polished brand content. The following strategy is designed to systematically encourage, collect, and leverage UGC for Rani Beauty Clinic.

### 4.1 In-Clinic Infrastructure

**The Selfie Station.** Designate a specific area of the clinic — ideally near the exit or in the post-treatment recovery area — as a dedicated photo opportunity. This space should feature excellent lighting (a large ring light or a well-lit window), a branded mirror, a subtle logo backdrop or branded wall element, and a small sign reading: *"You're glowing. Share it. Tag us @RaniBeautyClinic."* This passive prompt requires no staff effort and consistently generates organic content.

**Branded, Photogenic Packaging.** Every take-home item — aftercare bags, instruction cards, product samples, gift boxes — should be designed to be visually compelling enough to photograph and share. Beautiful packaging naturally inspires unboxing videos and product flat-lays. Ensure the clinic's social media handle and branded hashtag (e.g., **#RaniBeautyGlow**) are printed on all packaging.

**Staff Verbal Prompts.** Train all client-facing staff to gently encourage sharing at the appropriate moment. The most effective prompt occurs immediately after the client has seen their results in the mirror. The provider or treatment coordinator can say: *"Your skin looks incredible. If you take any photos or videos over the next few days, we'd love it if you tagged us — we share our favourites every week."*

### 4.2 Digital Incentive Programmes

**The Monthly "Tag to Win" Giveaway.** Run a continuous monthly competition where any client who posts a photo or video of their results and tags the clinic's account is automatically entered to win a complimentary service (e.g., a HydraFacial or a skincare product bundle). Announce the winner publicly each month to demonstrate that the giveaway is genuine and to encourage ongoing participation.

**Feature Friday.** Dedicate every Friday to featuring client UGC on the clinic's Instagram Stories and, with explicit permission, on the main Feed. People are highly motivated by the prospect of being featured by a brand they admire. Announce this programme clearly in the clinic and in the post-treatment email sequence.

**The Post-Treatment Email Sequence.** The automated follow-up email sent 3–5 days after the appointment (when results are typically most visible) should include a polite, warm request for a review and a photo. The email should provide clear, simple instructions: *"If you're loving your results, we'd be so grateful if you shared a photo and tagged us @RaniBeautyClinic using #RaniBeautyGlow. Your story could inspire someone else to take the first step."*

### 4.3 UGC Collection & Rights Management

Before reposting any client content, the clinic must obtain explicit permission. The standard industry protocol is to reply to the client's post or DM with a message such as: *"We absolutely love this! Would you mind if we shared your post on our page? We'll always credit you."* A "yes" reply constitutes consent for that specific repost. For ongoing use or paid advertising, a written release form is required.

All UGC should be logged in the **UGC Strategy Tracker** sheet in the Video Content Calendar Spreadsheet, recording the client, the content type, the consent obtained, and the date of reposting.

## Part 6: Equipment & Setup for Self-Produced Content

Maintaining a consistent flow of high-quality content between professional shoots requires a reliable, easy-to-use in-house setup. The following recommendations are calibrated for a clinic environment where content creation is a secondary activity to patient care, prioritising ease of use without sacrificing quality.

### 6.1 Camera & Capture

The most important principle for self-produced content is **consistency over perfection**. A modern smartphone with excellent lighting will consistently outperform a professional camera in poor lighting. The clinic should invest first in lighting, and second in camera equipment.

**Primary Device (Smartphone):** The Apple iPhone 15 Pro or Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra are the recommended primary capture devices for daily social media content. Both offer 4K video at 60fps, excellent optical stabilisation, and versatile lens systems (ultra-wide, wide, and telephoto) that cover the majority of content needs without any additional equipment.

**Secondary Device (Dedicated Camera):** For hero content — website imagery, paid advertising, and high-production Reels — a dedicated mirrorless camera such as the Sony ZV-E10 II or Panasonic Lumix G100 provides superior image quality, interchangeable lenses, and professional audio input. These cameras are specifically designed for content creators and offer a gentler learning curve than traditional DSLR cameras.

### 6.2 Lighting Setup

**The Ring Light (Essential).** A high-quality, large ring light (minimum 18 inches in diameter) is the single most impactful equipment investment for a beauty clinic. It provides even, flattering, shadow-free illumination that is ideal for talking-head videos, before/after photography, and product flat-lays. Select a bi-colour model (adjustable from warm to cool white) to match the room's ambient light.

**LED Panels (Recommended).** Two portable LED panels — one as a key light and one as a fill light — provide the flexibility to create professional, multi-directional lighting setups in any room of the clinic. Position the key light at a 45-degree angle to the subject and use the fill light to reduce shadows on the opposite side.

**Natural Light (Always Prioritise).** The clinic should identify the rooms and times of day that offer the best natural window light and schedule content creation sessions accordingly. A 5-in-1 reflector is an inexpensive tool that can dramatically improve natural light shots by bouncing light back onto the subject to fill shadows.

### 6.3 Audio

Clear, professional audio is as important as visual quality for educational and talking-head content. Poor audio is the most common reason viewers stop watching a video, regardless of how good the visuals are.

**Wireless Lavalier Microphone System (Essential).** The DJI Mic 2 or Rode Wireless GO II are the recommended systems for clinic use. Both clip easily onto the provider's collar, connect wirelessly to the smartphone or camera, and produce broadcast-quality audio. The DJI Mic 2 also features an on-board recording mode that provides a backup recording in case of connection issues.

### 6.4 Stabilisation

**Tripod (Essential).** A sturdy, adjustable tripod with a smartphone mount is required for all stationary shots. Look for a model with a ball head for quick angle adjustments and an articulating arm for overhead flat-lay shots.

**Smartphone Gimbal (Recommended).** For walk-through videos and any content that requires the camera to move, a smartphone gimbal (e.g., DJI Osmo Mobile 6) eliminates camera shake and produces smooth, cinematic footage that significantly elevates production value.

### 6.5 Post-Production Software

**Video Editing:** CapCut (free) is the recommended mobile editing application for Reels and TikToks. It offers an extensive library of trending templates, auto-captions, and effects specifically designed for short-form video. Adobe Premiere Rush is recommended for more complex edits.

**Photo Editing:** Adobe Lightroom Mobile (free tier available) allows the clinic to create and save custom editing presets that can be applied to every photo with a single tap, ensuring a consistent visual aesthetic across all feed content.

**Content Scheduling:** A dedicated scheduling tool such as Later, Planoly, or Buffer allows the team to plan, preview, and schedule posts in advance, ensuring the content calendar is executed consistently without requiring daily manual posting.

## Part 7: Compliance & Legal Considerations

All visual content produced for Rani Beauty Clinic must adhere to the following compliance requirements:

**Medical Advertising Standards.** Before-and-after images must be accompanied by appropriate disclaimers where required by local regulations (e.g., "Results may vary"). Claims about treatment outcomes must be accurate, substantiated, and not misleading.

**Patient Privacy (HIPAA / Local Equivalent).** No patient-identifiable information may appear in any content without explicit written consent. This includes names, faces, medical records, and any other identifying details.

**Platform-Specific Policies.** Instagram and TikTok have specific policies regarding the promotion of medical procedures, injectables, and before/after content. Review and adhere to the current platform guidelines before posting any treatment-related content, as policies are subject to change.

**Consent Documentation.** All signed consent forms must be stored securely and retained for a minimum of seven years, or as required by local law. Digital storage with a clear naming convention (e.g., `CONSENT_M001_[ClientName]_[Date].pdf`) is recommended.
