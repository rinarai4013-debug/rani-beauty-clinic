## 1. Insurance-Billable Services and CPT Codes

Not all services offered at Rani Beauty Clinic are eligible for insurance reimbursement. The fundamental rule is that a service must be **medically necessary** and documented as such in the patient's medical record. Cosmetic treatments — including Botox for aesthetics, dermal fillers, laser hair removal, and cosmetic laser resurfacing — are universally excluded from insurance coverage and must remain cash-pay. However, several of the clinic's medical services can be billed to insurance.

### Medical Consultations

Evaluation and Management (E/M) codes are the backbone of insurance billing for any medical practice. These codes are used for physician consultations and follow-up visits for weight management, hormone therapy, and other medical conditions.

| CPT Code | Description | When to Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 99203 | New patient, moderate complexity | Initial weight management or hormone consult |
| 99204 | New patient, high complexity | Complex new patient with multiple comorbidities |
| 99213 | Established patient, low-moderate complexity | Follow-up GLP-1 or hormone visit, 20 min |
| 99214 | Established patient, moderate complexity | Follow-up with prescription management, 30 min |

The distinction between 99213 and 99214 is based on the level of Medical Decision Making (MDM) or total time spent on the date of encounter. For most GLP-1 and hormone management visits, 99214 is appropriate when reviewing labs, adjusting medications, and managing comorbidities.

### GLP-1 Weight Management

GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., semaglutide/Wegovy, tirzepatide/Zepbound) for obesity are increasingly covered by commercial insurers, and Medicare is launching the "Medicare GLP-1 Bridge" program in 2026 to cover these medications for beneficiaries with obesity and qualifying comorbidities [1]. However, coverage is contingent on proper diagnosis coding.

The clinic must use specific ICD-10 codes for obesity (e.g., E66.01 for morbid obesity, E66.09 for other obesity) combined with qualifying comorbidities (e.g., E11.9 for Type 2 diabetes, I10 for hypertension). Billing "unspecified obesity" (E66.9) alone is frequently denied by commercial payers as of January 2026 [1]. The E/M visit (99213 or 99214) is billed for the consultation itself. If the clinic administers the GLP-1 injection on-site, **CPT 96372** (Therapeutic, prophylactic, or diagnostic injection, subcutaneous or intramuscular) is billed in addition to the E/M code [2].

### Hormone Therapy

Hormone replacement therapy is billable when medically necessary and supported by laboratory documentation. For testosterone therapy, this means documented low serum testosterone levels (generally below 300 ng/dL for males) with clinical symptoms.

| Code | Description | Payer Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| CPT 11980 | Subcutaneous hormone pellet implantation | Commercial & Medicare |
| HCPCS S0189 | Testosterone pellet, 75 mg | Private insurance |
| HCPCS J3145 | Injection, testosterone undecanoate, 1 mg | Medicare |
| CPT 96372 | Therapeutic injection (IM testosterone) | All payers |

Note that many commercial payers require prior authorization for testosterone therapy and pellet implantation. UnitedHealthcare, for example, has a specific medical policy for testosterone replacement therapy [3].

### Blood Work and Labs

If the clinic performs venipuncture and sends specimens to an outside reference laboratory (e.g., LabCorp, Quest), the clinic can bill for the draw. If the clinic processes labs in-house, it must hold a **CLIA Certificate of Waiver** (or higher complexity certificate) from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services.

| CPT Code | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| 36415 | Collection of venous blood by venipuncture |
| 80053 | Comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP) |
| 80061 | Lipid panel |
| 85014 | Hematocrit (required for testosterone monitoring) |
| 84403 | Testosterone, total |
| 84270 | Sex hormone binding globulin (SHBG) |

### Vitamin Injections (IM)

Intramuscular vitamin injections are billable to insurance only when there is a documented medical deficiency. For example, B12 injections are covered for pernicious anemia (ICD-10: D51.0) or B12 deficiency (E53.8). They are generally **not covered** for general wellness, fatigue, or elective purposes.

The administration code is **CPT 96372**, billed alongside the appropriate HCPCS drug code:
- **HCPCS J3420:** Injection, vitamin B-12 cyanocobalamin, up to 1000 mcg.

### What Is NOT Billable

The following services at Rani Beauty Clinic are cosmetic and must remain self-pay: Botox for cosmetic purposes, dermal fillers, cosmetic laser treatments, laser hair removal, and any service performed purely for aesthetic enhancement without a documented medical diagnosis.

## 2. Credentialing Steps for Dr. Landfield

Washington State law (RCW 48.43.750) requires all health carriers regulated by the Office of the Insurance Commissioner (OIC) to use a single state-designated electronic database for credentialing. As of 2024, that database is **CAQH ProView** [6]. The following steps must be completed in order.

### Step 1: Verify and Update NPI Numbers

Dr. Landfield must have an active **Type 1 (Individual) NPI** in the NPPES system. The clinic must also obtain a **Type 2 (Organization) NPI** for Rani Beauty Clinic as a billing entity. Both NPIs must reflect the current Renton practice address.

- **Portal:** https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/
- **Cost:** Free
- **Timeline:** Immediate if already registered; 1-2 business days for new registration

### Step 2: Confirm Washington State Medical License

Dr. Landfield's Washington State medical license must be active, unrestricted, and in good standing with the **Washington Medical Commission (WMC)**. The license must reflect the Renton practice address.

- **Verification:** https://wmc.wa.gov/licensing/verification-requests
- **Renewal Fee:** $411 per two-year cycle [7]
- **Renewal Cycle:** Every two years on or before the physician's birthday

### Step 3: Obtain or Renew DEA Registration

A DEA registration is required for prescribing controlled substances, including testosterone (Schedule III) and certain weight management medications. The DEA registration must list the Renton clinic address.

- **Portal:** https://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/drugreg/registration.html
- **Cost:** $888 for a three-year registration [8]
- **Timeline:** 4-6 weeks for new registration; renew online 60 days before expiration

### Step 4: Obtain Malpractice Insurance Certificate

Every insurer requires a current Certificate of Liability Insurance (also called the "face sheet" of the malpractice policy) covering the Renton location. Washington State requires a minimum of **$1 million per occurrence and $3 million annual aggregate** for professional liability [9].

- **Action:** Contact your malpractice carrier and request a certificate listing the Renton clinic address.
- **Timeline:** 1-3 business days from your carrier

### Step 5: Register and Complete CAQH ProView Profile

CAQH ProView is the central credentialing hub for all Washington State health plans. Registration is free for providers [10].

- **Portal:** https://proview.caqh.org/
- **Documents to Upload:** Medical license, DEA certificate, malpractice face sheet, current CV (last 5 years of work history in MM/YYYY format with all gaps explained), board certification, and NPI confirmation.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Attestation:** The profile must be re-attested every **120 days** to remain active. Failure to re-attest will result in the profile becoming inactive and insurers will be unable to pull your credentials.
- **Timeline to Complete:** Allow 1-2 weeks to gather all documents and complete the profile.

### Step 6: Enroll with Washington State Medicaid (Apple Health) — If Applicable

If the clinic intends to accept Medicaid-managed care plans (Molina Healthcare, Community Health Plan of WA, Coordinated Care/Ambetter), Dr. Landfield must first enroll with the **Washington State Health Care Authority (HCA)** as a Medicaid provider. This is a prerequisite for contracting with any Medicaid MCO in Washington.

- **Portal:** https://www.hca.wa.gov/billers-providers-partners/become-apple-health-provider/enroll-provider
- **Cost:** $750 application fee (as of January 1, 2026) [11]
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks

### Step 7: Apply to Individual Health Plans

Once the CAQH profile is complete and active, submit applications to each insurer (see Section 3 below). Most plans pull credentials directly from CAQH. Some require an additional supplemental application or contract request.

## 6. Business Entity and Tax ID Considerations

Rani Beauty Clinic does not necessarily need to create a new legal entity to accept insurance, but several important considerations apply.

The clinic must have a **Type 2 (Organization) NPI** and a distinct **Tax Identification Number (TIN)** or **Employer Identification Number (EIN)** registered with the IRS for billing purposes. Insurance claims are submitted under the organization's TIN, not Dr. Landfield's personal Social Security Number. Insurers like Regence require an IRS CP 575 or 147C letter to verify the Tax ID associated with the billing entity [6].

**Recommended structure for a medspa:** Many physician-supervised medspas in Washington operate with two distinct entities — a **Management Services Organization (MSO)** that owns the business assets (equipment, brand, staff) and a **Professional Service Corporation (PSC) or PLLC** that employs the physician and holds the medical practice. The PSC is the entity that contracts with insurers and receives insurance payments. This structure protects against the Corporate Practice of Medicine doctrine, which prohibits non-physicians from owning or controlling a medical practice in Washington. Consult a healthcare attorney familiar with Washington State law before establishing this structure.

If the clinic already operates as a PLLC or corporation with Dr. Landfield as an owner or employee, that existing entity's EIN can be used for insurance billing, provided it is properly enrolled with each payer.

## 7. Washington State Medspa Regulations for Insurance Billing

Washington State has specific regulations governing medspas that directly affect how insurance billing must be structured.

### Corporate Practice of Medicine

Washington State recognizes the Corporate Practice of Medicine doctrine, which restricts non-physicians from owning or controlling entities that practice medicine. This has direct implications for ownership structure and how insurance payments are received. The Washington Department of Health strongly recommends consulting an attorney when establishing a medspa business structure [16].

### Medical Director Requirements

All medspas in Washington must have a designated medical director who is a licensed physician (MD or DO) or a Physician Assistant (PA). Dr. Landfield, as a board-certified neurologist with an active Washington medical license, fulfills this requirement [16]. The WMC's Medical Directors Guideline (GUI2020-02, adopted August 21, 2020) outlines the medical director's responsibilities, including ensuring appropriate medical records are kept for each patient, maintaining patient confidentiality, and overseeing the scope of practice of all clinical staff.

### Supervision and Delegation Rules

The WMC has issued specific rules governing the delegation of medical procedures in a medspa setting:

- **WAC 246-919-605:** Governs laser use by MDs, including conditions under which procedures may be delegated to licensed professionals.
- **WAC 246-919-606:** Governs nonsurgical medical cosmetic procedures (injections, prescription devices) by MDs, including delegation and supervision requirements.

Under these rules, Dr. Landfield may delegate certain procedures to licensed clinical staff (e.g., RNs), but must provide appropriate supervision. The level of supervision required depends on the procedure and the licensee's qualifications [16].

### Medication Administration

Any person administering a medication (including IM vitamin injections) at the clinic must have legal authority to do so under their professional license. A medication order from Dr. Landfield is required prior to each administration. This applies to all injectable treatments, including B12, testosterone, and GLP-1 medications [16].

## 8. Billing Software Comparison

Selecting the right Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) software is one of the most consequential operational decisions for a small practice beginning to accept insurance. The software must handle claim submission, eligibility verification, denial management, and patient billing.

### Tebra (formerly Kareo)

Tebra is widely regarded as the best-in-class solution for solo practitioners and small independent practices. It combines RCM, scheduling, and patient engagement into a single cloud-based platform. A person with beginner-level technology knowledge can operate it effectively, which is important for a medspa staff that may not have prior medical billing experience [18].

- **Pricing:** $100 - $300 per provider per month (subscription model)
- **Key Features:** Automated denial management, real-time eligibility checks, automated patient statements, patient payment portal.
- **Pros:** Intuitive interface requiring minimal training; strong for self-pay and insurance hybrid practices.
- **Cons:** Premium features increase overall cost; some users report inconsistencies in customer support.

### AdvancedMD

AdvancedMD is designed for practices that anticipate growth and need deep customization. It offers reporting dashboards and modular add-ons for scheduling, billing, and clinical documentation [18].

- **Pricing:** Subscription or quote-based (typically higher than Tebra)
- **Key Features:** Highly customizable workflow templates, real-time revenue cycle visibility, scalable multi-provider architecture.
- **Pros:** Excellent for practices planning to add providers or locations; robust reporting.
- **Cons:** Higher initial cost; steeper learning curve; requires more staff training time.

### SimplePractice

SimplePractice is primarily designed for mental health and behavioral health providers. While it offers insurance billing, it lacks the depth of medical billing features needed for a medspa billing E/M codes, injection codes, and lab codes [19].

- **Pricing:** $29 - $99 per month (solo plans)
- **Key Features:** Integrated EHR, scheduling, telehealth, client portal.
- **Pros:** Very affordable; excellent for cash-pay practices with simple billing needs.
- **Cons:** Not optimized for medical billing complexity; limited claim scrubbing and denial management tools.

### CollaborateMD

CollaborateMD is a cloud-based practice management and billing solution that emphasizes claim accuracy, reporting a 99% first-pass claim acceptance rate. Pricing is based on claim volume, making it cost-effective for lower-volume practices [20].

- **Pricing:** Starts at $235 per month (per provider)
- **Key Features:** Real-time claim submission, Level 2 claim scrubbing (in Growth plan), unlimited ERA with auto-posting, 125+ customizable reports.
- **Pros:** Strong claim accuracy; flexible pricing tiers based on volume; good for small to medium practices.
- **Cons:** Interface can feel dated compared to newer platforms; no native EHR (requires integration).

## 9. Fee-for-Service vs. Capitated Contracts

When contracting with Washington State insurers, the clinic will encounter two primary payment models. Understanding the difference is essential for evaluating whether a contract is financially viable.

### Fee-for-Service (FFS)

Under a fee-for-service model, the insurer pays the clinic a negotiated rate for each specific service or procedure performed, identified by CPT code. For example, the clinic submits a claim for a 99214 visit, and the insurer pays the contracted rate for that code. FFS is the standard model for specialists and medspas, and it is what the clinic will encounter with Premera, Regence, Aetna, Cigna, and UHC.

The key advantage of FFS is transparency: the clinic knows exactly what it will be paid for each service. The key risk is that high-volume, low-complexity services (like routine injection visits) may not be reimbursed at rates that cover the clinic's overhead.

### Capitation

Under a capitated model, the insurer pays the clinic a fixed per-member, per-month (PMPM) amount to provide a defined set of services to an enrolled panel of patients, regardless of how many times those patients visit. Capitation is most common in primary care settings and HMO plans like Kaiser Permanente. As a specialty medspa, Rani Beauty Clinic will almost certainly not be offered capitated contracts. However, if Kaiser WA approaches the clinic with a capitated arrangement, it is important to carefully model the expected utilization against the PMPM rate before signing.

**For Rani Beauty Clinic:** Pursue fee-for-service contracts exclusively. Do not accept capitated arrangements without a thorough actuarial analysis.

## 10. Incident-To Billing: Can Rina Bill Under Dr. Landfield?

"Incident-to" billing is a Medicare provision that allows a clinic to bill for services performed by auxiliary personnel (e.g., an RN, LPN, or Medical Assistant) under the physician's NPI, receiving 100% of the physician fee schedule rate rather than the reduced rate that would apply if the NPP billed independently [17].

### Requirements for Incident-To Billing

For a service to qualify as incident-to under Medicare, all of the following conditions must be met [17]:

1. The service must be an integral part of the patient's normal course of treatment.
2. The physician (Dr. Landfield) must have personally performed the **initial service** and established the treatment plan.
3. The physician must remain **actively involved** in the patient's ongoing care.
4. The physician must provide **direct supervision** — meaning Dr. Landfield must be physically present in the office suite and immediately available to provide assistance while the auxiliary staff member performs the treatment.
5. The service must be commonly provided in the physician's office or clinic.

### Application at Rani Beauty Clinic

If Rina (an esthetician or nurse) administers a B12 injection or draws blood for labs, this can be billed incident-to Dr. Landfield's NPI provided Dr. Landfield is physically present in the clinic at the time of service and has established the treatment plan. The claim is submitted under Dr. Landfield's NPI and the clinic's TIN.

**Critical limitation — Estheticians:** Licensed estheticians in Washington State have a defined scope of practice limited to cosmetic skin care. They cannot administer injections or perform medical procedures. Only licensed clinical staff (RNs, LPNs, MAs under physician supervision) can administer IM injections. If Rina is an esthetician, she cannot administer medical injections regardless of incident-to billing rules.

**Commercial payers:** Most commercial insurers follow Medicare's incident-to rules, but the specific requirements vary by payer contract. Always review the payer's provider manual to confirm incident-to billing is permitted under your specific contract.

**2026 Update:** CMS made permanent a rule allowing virtual direct supervision for incident-to services in certain circumstances (e.g., telehealth), effective 2026 [17]. However, for in-office injection administration, physical presence remains the standard.
