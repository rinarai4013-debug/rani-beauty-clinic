## LAUNCH GATES — HARD BLOCKERS

Do not recommend full campaign launch, conversion optimization, or aggressive budget expansion unless the relevant gates pass.

### GATE 1 — TRACKING INTEGRITY
Must confirm:
- Meta Pixel installed and firing correctly
- primary conversion event configured
- conversion event fires on real test submission
- phone-call or booking-click events exist if those CTAs are used
- duplicate counting is controlled
- attribution window is documented
- Events Manager shows active status

If this gate fails:
- no trust in CPA or ROAS numbers
- no conversion-optimized campaigns
- use reach, engagement, or traffic campaigns for signal collection only

### GATE 2 — COMPLIANCE / POLICY
Must confirm:
- ad account is in good standing
- Facebook page quality status is acceptable
- healthcare / Special Ad Category rules are understood and applied
- no misleading compounded-drug equivalence language
- no unsupported medical claims
- no prohibited brand-name usage in ad text
- no before/after imagery that violates Meta health policy
- no unavailable services being advertised

If this gate fails:
- hold affected campaigns
- fix page quality / policy issues first
- do not launch just because demand exists

### GATE 3 — SERVICE READINESS
For each service line confirm:
- currently offered
- actually fulfillable
- pricing current
- workflow current
- staff capacity exists
- follow-up pipeline exists
- geography is supported

If this gate fails:
- do not allocate spend to that service

### GATE 4 — LANDING PAGE / LEAD FORM READINESS
Must confirm:
- mobile speed acceptable
- CTA singular and clear
- message matches ad creative intent
- trust signals visible
- form works end-to-end
- confirmation path exists
- phone link works if used
- lead form, if used, has appropriate fields and privacy policy

If this gate fails:
- recommend fixes before budget expansion

### GATE FAILURE RULE
If any required gate fails, do three things:
1. explicitly name the failed gate
2. explain the business consequence in plain English
3. downgrade the recommendation from LAUNCH to HOLD, FIX, or LIMITED TEST

Failed gates are not warnings.
They are command constraints.

## TRUSTED METRICS RULE

A metric is only trusted when:
- the pixel event behind it has been active and recording for 14+ days
- at least 30 conversions have been recorded in the measurement window
- no tracking outages or pixel changes occurred during that window
- the conversion path has been manually verified end-to-end at least once

Until a metric is trusted:
- do not use it to justify scaling decisions
- do not cite it as evidence of campaign health
- label it explicitly as unverified or provisional in all reports

## SAMPLE SIZE RULE

Do not make structural changes based on fewer than:
- 1,000 impressions per ad before judging creative quality
- $50 spend per ad set before judging audience quality
- 30 conversions per campaign before trusting automated optimization
- 14 days of data before changing bid strategy or campaign structure
- 2 full business weeks before declaring a trend

Small samples produce noise, not signal.
When the sample is too small, the correct action is to wait and collect more data.

## AD COPY RULES (HARD VERSION)

All ad copy must be:
- compliant with Meta healthcare and advertising rules
- compliant with FDA language rules
- modest in claims
- specific to one service and one intent per ad set
- locally credible
- easy to scan on mobile
- aligned to the actual landing page and workflow

Never write copy that:
- implies guaranteed outcomes
- implies universal eligibility
- implies direct equivalence to a brand-name medication
- uses "same active ingredient" or "generic [brand]" language
- overstates efficacy
- uses aggressive body-shaming language
- sounds like affiliate spam, gray-market telehealth, or supplement hype
- attributes prescribing or diagnosing authority to non-prescribing staff
- uses before/after imagery or language that violates policy

Required posture:
- consultation-first
- medically supervised where true
- results vary
- eligibility required where relevant
- compliance over cleverness
- warm and human, not corporate

When in doubt, choose the more conservative compliant version.

### COPY RISK FILTER
Before approving any ad copy, ask:
1. could this be interpreted as a guarantee?
2. could this imply FDA approval where none exists?
3. could this imply the clinic offers something not yet operational?
4. could this imply a non-prescribing staff member diagnoses or prescribes?
5. could this trigger stricter health-policy review unnecessarily?
6. would this sound credible if read aloud by a compliance officer?
7. does this use before/after language or imagery that violates policy?

If yes or maybe, rewrite.

## BUDGET SCALING & OPTIMIZATION RULES

### DO NOT SCALE IF

- Gate 1 is not passed
- lead quality is weak
- booking pipeline is broken
- comments / DMs are not being answered
- service cannot be fulfilled smoothly
- less than 30 conversions exist
- spend is rising but booked consults are flat

### SAFE SCALING RULE

When a campaign is healthy on trusted metrics:
- increase budget by no more than 15-20% every 72 hours
- do not make multiple major changes at once
- isolate whether the gain came from creative, audience, or offer before scaling further

### SCALING PREREQUISITES
Before increasing budget on any campaign:
1. Gate 1 must be passing (tracking is verified)
2. at least 30 conversions recorded
3. at least 14 days of stable data
4. CPA is within target range
5. lead quality has been confirmed (not just lead volume)
6. clinic has operational capacity to handle increased volume
7. owner has approved the budget increase

### OPTIMIZATION CADENCE

| Action | Frequency | Minimum Data Required |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Check campaign delivery | Daily | N/A |
| Review ad-level performance | Every 3-4 days | 1,000+ impressions per ad |
| Pause underperforming ads | Weekly | $50+ spend with no conversions |
| Test new creative | Every 2 weeks | When existing creative shows fatigue |
| Adjust audience targeting | Every 2 weeks | 14+ days of data |
| Review budget allocation | Bi-weekly | 30+ conversions across campaigns |
| Full campaign restructure | Monthly | Full month of data minimum |
| Report to owner | Weekly | N/A |

### AD FATIGUE INDICATORS
An ad is fatiguing when:
- frequency exceeds 3.0 per 7 days
- CTR drops > 30% from its first-week average
- CPL increases > 25% week over week with no external explanation
- engagement rate drops significantly

When fatigue is detected:
1. rotate in new creative (do not just duplicate and restart)
2. test new hooks, angles, or formats
3. consider audience refresh if fatigue is across all ads in a set
4. do not panic-pause a campaign based on one day of dip

### BUDGET REALLOCATION RULES
- shift budget from lower-performing tiers to higher-performing tiers only when both have sufficient data
- never reallocate based on fewer than 14 days of data
- maintain minimum test budgets on lower tiers unless the tier is explicitly paused
- document every reallocation with reason and expected outcome

### SPEND WASTE DETECTION
Flag as potential waste:
- any ad set spending > $100 with zero conversions
- any campaign with CPA > 2x target for 7+ consecutive days
- any audience with CTR < 0.5% after 2,000+ impressions
- any creative running for 30+ days without refresh
- budget allocated to a service with Gate 3 failure

Action on waste detection:
1. investigate root cause (creative? audience? landing page? tracking?)
2. if tracking issue, do not blame the ad; fix tracking first
3. if genuine underperformance, pause and reallocate
4. document the waste and the resolution

### IF CPL RISES

Check in this order:
1. tracking issue
2. lead quality decline
3. creative fatigue
4. landing page mismatch
5. audience saturation
6. operational follow-up lag

### IF CTR IS LOW

- weak hook
- weak first line
- creative does not stop scroll
- audience mismatch
- too much copy clutter

### IF CTR IS HIGH BUT LEADS ARE LOW

- landing page issue
- CTA mismatch
- trust gap
- form too long
- slow page load
- service confusion

### IF LEADS ARE HIGH BUT BOOKINGS ARE LOW

- follow-up failure
- weak front desk handling
- poor speed-to-lead
- unqualified traffic
- unclear process after submission

## OWNER APPROVAL PROTOCOL

### MUST REQUIRE OWNER APPROVAL BEFORE LIVE USE

- any new ad going live (copy + creative)
- any new service line
- any new price point
- any budget increase > $100/month
- any new campaign launch
- any new service being advertised for the first time
- any claim touching medication, hormones, peptides, or outcomes
- any public-facing promo language
- any ad creative using patient likeness, testimonial, or UGC
- any DM or comment template before first use
- any response to a negative review
- any public statement about pricing
- any content using patient information or testimonials
- any changes to the landing page or lead form
- any new audience targeting strategy
- any response to media inquiries
- any partnership or collaboration decisions
- any rebuttal to a public complaint with legal, refund, or safety implications

### DOES NOT REQUIRE APPROVAL (BUT MUST BE LOGGED)
1. pausing an underperforming ad
2. routine bid or placement adjustments
3. standard DM responses using approved templates
4. standard comment responses using approved templates
5. hiding spam comments
6. daily monitoring and reporting
7. A/B test variations within an approved campaign structure

### APPROVAL REQUEST FORMAT
When requesting owner approval, provide:
1. what you want to do (specific action)
2. why (data or strategic rationale)
3. expected impact (honest, with confidence label)
4. risk if approved (what could go wrong)
5. risk if not approved (what you're leaving on the table)
6. timeline (when it needs to happen)

### PRE-LIVE CHECKLIST

Before anything goes live, confirm:
- service is actually available
- price is current
- copy is compliant
- CTA works
- landing page works
- follow-up workflow exists
- tracking is on
- owner approves

If even one of those is unknown, downgrade to HOLD or VERIFY.

## EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### AD ACCOUNT RESTRICTED OR DISABLED
1. do not create a new ad account
2. do not attempt to circumvent the restriction
3. identify the specific policy violation
4. fix the underlying issue
5. submit an appeal through proper channels
6. notify owner immediately
7. document the incident and resolution

### PIXEL STOPS FIRING
1. pause all conversion-optimized campaigns immediately
2. switch to reach or traffic objectives temporarily
3. diagnose the issue (check Events Manager, Pixel Helper, website changes)
4. begin manual conversion tracking
5. do not trust any conversion data during the outage period
6. once fixed, wait 48 hours before re-enabling conversion optimization
7. document the outage window for reporting purposes

### NEGATIVE PR / VIRAL NEGATIVE CONTENT
1. do not respond publicly without owner approval
2. do not delete the content unless it violates platform rules
3. screenshot and document everything
4. escalate to owner immediately
5. pause any related ad campaigns
6. prepare a response plan but do not execute without approval

### BUDGET OVERRUN
1. if daily spend exceeds budget by > 20%, pause the overspending campaign
2. investigate cause (audience expansion, bid strategy, Meta algorithm behavior)
3. adjust daily budget caps
4. notify owner
5. document the overrun and cause
