# Injectable vs. Oral Peptides: Which Delivery Method Is Right for You?

Peptide therapy has become one of the most sought-after tools in regenerative medicine, and for good reason. From improved sleep and recovery to enhanced energy and cellular repair, peptides offer targeted biological benefits that are difficult to achieve through lifestyle changes alone.

But if you have started researching peptide therapy, you have probably noticed that the same peptide often comes in different forms. Sermorelin, for example, can be administered as a subcutaneous injection or taken as an oral troche (a small lozenge that dissolves under your tongue). NAD+ is available the same way.

So which delivery method is actually better? The honest answer is that it depends on your goals, your comfort level, your budget, and how quickly you want to see results.

## Understanding the Two Delivery Methods

### Injectable Peptides

Injectable peptides are administered via small subcutaneous injections, typically using a very fine insulin-style needle. The injection goes just beneath the skin, usually in the abdomen or thigh. Most patients describe it as a minor pinch that becomes routine very quickly.

**Why injectables have an edge in bioavailability:** When a peptide is injected directly into subcutaneous tissue, it enters the bloodstream without passing through the digestive system. This means a higher percentage of the active compound reaches your cells in its intended form. For peptides that are dose-sensitive, this matters.

### Oral Troches

Troches are small lozenges that dissolve slowly under the tongue (sublingual absorption). This delivery method allows the peptide to enter the bloodstream through the thin mucosal tissue in the mouth, partially bypassing the digestive system.

Troches are an excellent option for patients who prefer to avoid needles or want a simpler daily routine. They are easy to store, require no preparation, and can be taken discreetly at any time.

## NAD+: Injectable vs. Oral

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every living cell. It plays a critical role in energy production, DNA repair, and cellular aging. NAD+ levels decline naturally with age, which is one reason why fatigue, cognitive decline, and slower recovery become more common as we get older.

Supplementing NAD+ has been shown to support mental clarity, sustained energy, improved mood, and healthier aging at the cellular level.

## Who Should Choose Injectables?

Injectable peptides may be the better fit if you:

- Have specific, measurable health or performance goals
- Want the highest possible bioavailability from each dose
- Are comfortable with self-administered subcutaneous injections
- Prefer a 3-month supply that reduces the need for frequent reorders
- Have used peptides before and want to optimize your protocol

## Who Should Choose Oral Troches?

Oral troches may be the better fit if you:

- Prefer to avoid needles entirely
- Are new to peptide therapy and want to start with a lower commitment
- Value simplicity and convenience in your daily health routine
- Want an affordable entry point into regenerative medicine
- Plan to combine peptide therapy with other oral wellness protocols
