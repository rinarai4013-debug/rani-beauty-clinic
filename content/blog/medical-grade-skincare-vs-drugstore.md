## Why Drugstore Products Cannot Penetrate Deep Enough

Your skin is designed to be a barrier. Its outermost layer, the stratum corneum, is built to keep things out. That is exactly what it does when you apply most over-the-counter skincare products.

Drugstore products are formulated within FDA cosmetic guidelines, which means they are limited in the concentration of active ingredients they can contain. They are also typically designed with larger molecular structures that sit on the surface of the skin rather than penetrating to the deeper dermal layers where collagen, elastin, and new skin cells are produced.

In practical terms, most drugstore serums and creams hydrate the surface, create a temporary smoothing effect, and then wash off. They are not designed to change the structure or function of your skin. They legally cannot make those claims, because they are not formulated to do so.

## How Prescription Compounds Work Differently

Prescription skincare operates under a completely different set of rules. These products are formulated by compounding pharmacists, prescribed by licensed providers, and designed with active ingredients at concentrations that can penetrate the skin barrier and create real, measurable change in the deeper layers where it matters.

The key differences include:

- **Higher active ingredient concentrations** that exceed what cosmetic regulations allow
- **Smaller molecular weights** engineered to pass through the stratum corneum
- **Medical-grade delivery systems** that carry active ingredients to the dermal layer
- **Physician oversight** to ensure the right formulation for your specific skin concerns

When a prescription compound reaches the dermis, it can stimulate collagen production, accelerate cell turnover, inhibit melanin production, and trigger genuine tissue repair. That is the difference between a product that makes your skin feel soft and a product that makes your skin fundamentally healthier.

## The Active Ingredients That Make the Difference

Not all prescription ingredients are created equal. Here are the key actives that form the foundation of our Rx skincare program at Rani Beauty Clinic.

### GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring peptide that plays a critical role in wound healing, collagen synthesis, and tissue remodeling. In prescription topical form, GHK-Cu has been shown to improve skin firmness, reduce fine lines, and support the skin's natural repair processes. It is one of the most studied peptides in dermatological research, and its ability to tighten and remodel skin makes it a standout active for patients concerned with laxity and aging.

### Retinoids

Retinoids (vitamin A derivatives) are the gold standard of anti-aging skincare, and for good reason. Prescription-strength retinoids accelerate cell turnover, boost collagen production, reduce hyperpigmentation, and smooth texture. Over-the-counter retinol products contain a fraction of the active concentration found in prescription formulations, which is why they produce a fraction of the results.

### NAD+ (Topical)

NAD+ is a coenzyme essential for cellular energy production and DNA repair. When applied topically in a medical-grade formulation, NAD+ supports skin cell vitality, promotes a healthy, radiant complexion, and may help combat the visible effects of cellular aging. It is a newer addition to prescription skincare, and its potential is significant.

### Hydroquinone

Hydroquinone remains the most effective topical treatment for hyperpigmentation, melasma, and uneven skin tone. Prescription-strength hydroquinone inhibits tyrosinase, the enzyme responsible for melanin production, and can significantly brighten and even out skin tone when used under physician supervision. Over-the-counter alternatives are limited to concentrations that are often insufficient for meaningful correction.
