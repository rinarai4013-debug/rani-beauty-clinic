## What Is Tirzepatide and How Does It Work?

Tirzepatide belongs to a new class of medications that works on two hormonal pathways simultaneously. Unlike older GLP-1 receptor agonists (like semaglutide) that target only one pathway, tirzepatide is a dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist. That dual mechanism is what makes it different.

**GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1)** helps regulate blood sugar, slows gastric emptying so you feel full longer, and reduces appetite at the brain level.

**GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide)** enhances the body's insulin response and plays a direct role in fat metabolism. When GIP and GLP-1 work together, the result is more significant appetite reduction, better metabolic function, and greater fat loss than targeting either pathway alone.

In simple terms: tirzepatide tells your brain you are not hungry, tells your stomach to take its time, and tells your metabolism to work more efficiently. The combination is powerful.
