### The Clinical Problem

Patients on GLP-1 receptor agonists (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Liraglutide) frequently experience secondary dermatological effects including:

- **Skin laxity** — loss of dermal volume support due to rapid subcutaneous fat reduction
- **Facial volume loss** — hollowing of the malar and buccal fat pads ("Ozempic face")
- **Textural changes** — decreased skin elasticity, crepey appearance
- **Body contour changes** — excess skin on abdomen, arms, medial thighs

These concerns can impact patient quality of life, body image, and treatment adherence.

---

### Our Approach

Rani Beauty Clinic offers a **physician-supervised, non-surgical skin restoration protocol** for your patients experiencing skin changes during or after GLP-1 therapy.

**The Reveal Protocol** combines two FDA-cleared technologies:

| Technology | Mechanism | Depth | Primary Indication |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| **Sofwave** | SUPERB ultrasound | 1.5mm (mid-dermis) | Collagen/elastin stimulation, tissue lifting |
| **Secret RF** | Fractional radiofrequency microneedling | 0.5–3.5mm (adjustable) | Dermal remodeling, texture improvement |

**Why dual-technology:** Post-GLP-1 skin laxity involves both deep collagen framework loss and superficial dermal changes. Single-modality treatments address only one layer. Our protocol targets both simultaneously for more comprehensive restoration.

---

### Patient Eligibility

Ideal referral candidates:
- Weight loss of 30+ lbs on GLP-1 therapy
- Moderate to significant skin laxity (face, neck, or body)
- Not a candidate for surgical excision (or prefers non-surgical approach)
- No active skin infections or uncontrolled autoimmune conditions
- Can be actively on GLP-1 medication (no discontinuation required)

**Contraindications:** Active skin infection at treatment site, pregnancy, implanted electrical devices at treatment site, keloid tendency (relative)
